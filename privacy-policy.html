Privacy Policy

Friday, 5 Feb 2021

Introduction

This privacy notice is a formal reminder regarding our collection and handling of personal data and other forms of information relating to your privacy, that you share with us. We take your privacy very seriously and take measures that comply with the laws to safeguard and protect the data you share with us and the data we process or store.

We comply with the Korean legislative system for personal information protection and therefore are in compliance with the Personal Information Protection Act (“PIPA”). Unless granted permission, we do not share, sell, or disclose to any third party, any information collected through our website or through the use of our services.

1. Handling your data

We may collect, transfer, store or share different kinds of personal data you share with us. This includes Information like your first name, last name, date of birth, job title and other data sets that can be used to identify you.

We may also collect your contact information such as your home, office or billing address, email address, contact numbers and any other information provided that allows us to communicate with you.

When you purchase services from our platform, we may collect transactional data and with it, details about payments regarding the products and service we have provided you.

Marketing data includes survey forms, application forms found on our website, email campaigns and other such marketing collateral that you may have opted in for whilst using our services.

Other areas where you may be required to share information with us would include:

Telephone calls

Personal meetings

Video-conferencing

Project management software

2. Local jurisdiction

In some rare cases, we may be required to share information, data or any other materials should they be required by local authorities during a pending case. In this event, you will be notified of such an occurrence. Please note we are operating under the Korean Republic jurisdiction and are subject to local laws.

3. Identity Verification

Upon signing a contractual agreement with EastFort and in order to carry out the requirements under the contract terms, we may use the data in a number of ways. We may need to verify your identity for security screening purposes and as part of a wider KYC process. We process this information on the grounds that there is a mutual agreement between us, by way of a signed contract. The processing of your information will continue until the services have been completed and signed off or you request that we terminate your data and information. This would revoke your access to our platform and services and would permit you from using our website.

4. Payments & transactions

Payment for services are transferred directly via bank transfer. Our employees, any third party freelancer or sub-contractor does not have access to your banking information.

5. Dispute Resolution

In the event of a dispute we withhold and retain all the information provided until such time as a resolution has been made. This information is used only in regards to a dispute resolution.

In some cases, we are required to use this information with third parties that may be involved in the resolution process. It is at our discretion what information is shared and this ensures a smoother, faster and simpler resolution is made.

6. Cookies

Our website uses cookies in conjunction with your browser. This is in order to provide a much more fluid experience with our services. Cookies may remain on your system however, you retain the right to delete the cookies at any stage.

When you open our website, we will ask you if you consent to the use of cookies. If you decline, the only data recorded will be your site visit history.

If you choose not to use cookies or you alter your browser settings to disable their use and prevent their use through your browser settings, you may have a reduced experience with our services.

Cookies maybe used by us under these conditions:

Website event history

Form submissions

Live chat

7. Third Parties

Although we do not disclose your personal information to any third party (except when clearly defined and consented to under contractual agreement relating to our services) , we sometimes take data from third parties that may have some relation to your personal information. A good example of this would be collaboration software. This data is not shared, stored or used in any other way other than to identify you.

8. Change requests

If you change your personal information, such as your name, gender, address or workplace, It is important to let us know so we can edit the documentation and keep your records up to date. This is a legal requirement should there be any pending case at a court of law or such time as an authority requests to review your information.

9. Access to your personal information

If you wish to amend any of the personal identifiable information we may be holding during a contract or service, please send our team a message so we can take appropriate action.

10. Deletion of your personal information

You may request at any stage that we are to delete all of your information and this will be done so diligently and ethically. This may however limit your experience with us and may provide you with a reduced service.

If you would like all of your information wiped, please contact us and we will be happy to help with your request.

11. Under age limitations

We do not sell market to or sell products or services to anyone under the age of 18. The use of our website is open freely to anyone but booking services would require a legal guardian’s permission, should the stakeholder be under age.

12. SSL security

Data transferred between both parties is performed using Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) certificates which are used as a security method to verify our identity and to encrypt your data. To confirm this is correctly in use, and for best practice, please look out for the padlock key found next to your url.

13. Holding your data

Unless specified otherwise, your data will be kept under the following conditions:

For the duration of service until such as time as the contract has ended, expired, a dispute has been raised or if we are required to do so by the law.

14. Legal compliance

Our privacy policy has been revised in order to comply with the law of the Republic of Korea. We also safeguard ourselves and our clients by complying with any legal jurisdiction where we choose to operate.

15. Policy updates and amendments

From time to time, we will be required to amend, update and review this policy. All updates will be edited on this page and any critical information will be sent to you should it affect you and should you have consented to the relevant communication. Please keep an eye on this page for updates and amendments by looking at the, ‘last updated’ date field at the top of the page.

If you would like to speak with us about your privacy rights, data protection or any compliance issue, please feel free to contact us.
